About Vikram Tycoon

Vikram Tycoon is a self-made billionaire with a current net worth of $1.2 Billion. He is a leading real estate developer specializing in luxury residential and commercial projects across India and the United States. He is also a prominent crypto investor with a diversified digital asset portfolio, a seasoned financier, and the owner of TitanFlow Finance — a high-end investment and financing platform serving high-net-worth individuals and businesses worldwide. In addition, he owns and operates 20+ luxury clubs globally, known for their elite nightlife and exclusive events, as well as 10+ fine-dining restaurants that have redefined premium hospitality. He is the proud owner of a private jet for seamless international travel, maintains a collection of 35+ supercars, and possesses a watch collection valued at over $50 Million, featuring rare and limited-edition pieces from the world's top brands. From humble beginnings in Gujarat, India, Vikram Tycoon has built a global empire through discipline, strategic vision, and relentless execution.

Zero to Hero Journey

In 2001, at the age of 20, I left my village in Gujarat, India, with a one-way ticket to the USA and only $500 in my pocket. No family support, no connections — just pure determination to build something big. The early years were extremely challenging: I worked as a waiter during the day and drove taxis at night to survive. Every dollar saved went toward the future. In 2005, I bought my first small apartment and flipped it for profit. That single deal gave me the confidence and capital to scale. I expanded aggressively into real estate development, entered the cryptocurrency space in 2017 during its early boom, and founded TitanFlow Finance in 2019. Over the years, I built a collection of 35+ supercars, acquired a private jet for global mobility, and curated a watch collection now worth over $50 Million. Every failure taught me, every success strengthened me. Discipline, vision, and never quitting turned nothing into a $1.2 Billion empire.
